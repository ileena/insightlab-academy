## Introduction

Engagement shows **whether value repeats**; retention shows **whether users stay** long enough for compounding. Together they explain product-market fit more clearly than vanity top-of-funnel counts.

---

## 5.1 – Engagement Metrics

### Why This Matters

Healthy products show **habitual use** of core workflows—not one-off spikes from campaigns. Engagement metrics detect slipping habits before cancellations appear.

### Explanation

Useful engagement families:

- **Breadth** — DAU, WAU, MAU; stickiness (DAU/MAU).  
- **Depth** — sessions per user, actions per session, key events per week.  
- **Feature-level adoption** — % of actives touching new capabilities (with ideal user profiles in mind).  
- **Collaboration metrics** — invites accepted, shared artifacts, comments—when network value matters.

Avoid **engagement for engagement’s sake**: scrolling time might be negative value if users are lost. Tie metrics to **task completion** or **outcomes** where possible.

### Example

A analytics SaaS product launches a **natural-language query** feature. **Trials among 10% of weekly actives** sounds low, but those users run **3× more saved reports** and convert **2×** to paid—engagement depth in a *high-value slice* informs packaging and education, not only headline adoption %.

### Practical Takeaway

Segment engagement by **persona and lifecycle stage**; global averages often tell you to “improve engagement” without saying where.

## 5.2 – Retention Analysis

### Why This Matters

Retention curves reveal **when** users leave and **which cohorts** diverge—far richer than a single churn percentage at the company level.

### Explanation

**Retention analysis** typically uses **cohort tables**: rows are signup weeks, columns are periods since signup, cells show % still **active** under your definition.

Techniques:

- **N-day vs unbounded retention** — fixed horizon clarity vs long-run survival.  
- **Cohort quality controls** — distinguish organic vs incentive-driven signups.  
- **Leading indicators** — early session frequency often predicts later retention; use for faster feedback.  
- **Resurrection** — returning churned users; track separately from never-churned to avoid muddying narratives.

Product analytics tools automate curves; the skill is **choosing the active definition** that matches value, not mere opens.

### Example

Cohort tables show **EU SMB** accounts retain well after **week 3** if they **invite ≥2 collaborators**; those who do not invite rarely recover. In-app prompts move from generic “invite friends” to **role-based collaborator suggestions** tied to project templates—week-3 retention for that slice improves.

### Practical Takeaway

Every retention report should ship with **one behavioral split** (not ten) that the team will act on this month—otherwise analysis stalls in interesting but unfocused slices.

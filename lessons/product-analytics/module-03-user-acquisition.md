## Introduction

Acquisition brings users into the top of the funnel. Product analytics contributes by attributing signups to **channels**, measuring **awareness-to-intent** progression, and flagging when marketing-driven volume **diverges in quality** from organic users.

---

## 3.1 – Acquisition Channels

### Why This Matters

Not all traffic is equal. Channel analysis tells you where to invest, which onboarding paths to build, and which partnerships **activate** versus merely **register**.

### Explanation

Common **acquisition channels** include:

- **Paid search and social** — measurable CPC/CPA; sensitive to creative and landing-page match.  
- **Organic search** — intent-heavy; depends on content and technical SEO.  
- **Direct and brand** — navigational traffic; often high intent but hard to influence short term.  
- **Referral and viral** — invites, word of mouth; can scale non-linearly if loops exist.  
- **Sales-assisted** (B2B) — outbound, events, partners—often tracked in CRM rather than only web analytics.

Product analytics links channels (via UTM parameters, self-reported signup fields, or modeled attribution) to **downstream activation and retention**, not only click counts.

### Example

Two campaigns both report **$40 CPA**. Channel A users show **35% activation**; Channel B **18%**. Blended CPA hides that A is more efficient on a **quality-adjusted** basis; budget shifts while product improves onboarding for B’s audience if strategic.

### Practical Takeaway

Define a **standard acquisition taxonomy** (UTM conventions, default channel grouping rules) and audit it quarterly—broken tags silently misroute millions in spend decisions.

## 3.2 – Awareness Metrics

### Why This Matters

Down-funnel metrics dominate product reviews, but **awareness** metrics explain whether you have a demand ceiling. They connect product positioning to the size of the addressable funnel.

### Explanation

**Awareness metrics** sit high in the customer journey: impressions, reach, branded search volume, site visits, content views, or **share of voice** proxies in social listening. They are often marketing-owned but should feed product positioning decisions.

Caveats:

- **Viewability and bot traffic** distort impressions; use platform-reported quality filters and sanity checks.  
- **Lift studies** (geo holdouts, conversion lift) help connect awareness spend to incremental signups where clean attribution is impossible.

Product teams use awareness diagnostics when **conversion and retention are healthy** but **top-of-funnel volume** stalls—pointing to positioning, category creation, or distribution—not another onboarding tweak.

### Example

A niche B2B tool sees stable **trial-to-paid** and strong **90-day retention**, but **inbound demo requests** flatten. Web analytics shows **non-brand organic sessions** declining. Product marketing invests in **comparison pages and integration guides**, while product ships **public API docs** that rank—awareness work tied to **search-intent** content.

### Practical Takeaway

Pick **one or two awareness metrics** that historically lead your funnel by 1–2 months in your data; ignore the rest on executive slides to avoid noise.
